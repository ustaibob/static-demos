Little's Law

The universal relationship between inventory, flow rate, and flow time. Holds for any stable system.

I = R × T

Parameters

Average number of units entering (and leaving) the system per unit time.
Flow Time (T) 3.0 hr
Average time a unit spends inside the system (wait + service).

Kingman's Equation

Waiting time is driven by three multiplicative factors: capacity, utilization, and variability.

Tq = (p/m) × [u/(1-u)] × [(CVa²+CVp²)/2]

Parameters

Service Time (p, min) 10 min
Average time to serve one customer.
Number of Servers (m) 2
Parallel servers (e.g., checkout lanes).
Utilization (u) 75%
Fraction of time servers are busy. Drag toward 100% to see the explosion.
Arrival CV (CVa) 1.0
Coefficient of variation of inter-arrival times. 0 = perfectly regular, 1 = Poisson.
Service CV (CVp) 1.0
Coefficient of variation of service times. 0 = constant service, 1 = exponential.
Adjust utilization to see the nonlinear penalty.

Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)

The order size that minimizes total annual inventory cost by balancing setup and holding costs.

EOQ = √(2KR/h)  |  TC = (R/Q)·K + (Q/2)·h

Parameters

Annual Demand (R) 1,200 units/yr
Total units demanded per year.
Setup / Order Cost (K) $200
Fixed cost incurred each time an order is placed.
Annual Holding Cost (h) $8 / unit / yr
Cost to hold one unit in inventory for one year (storage, capital, obsolescence).
Adjust parameters to see the cost crossover.

Safety Stock & Reorder Point

Safety stock is a buffer against demand uncertainty. The ROP triggers replenishment while the buffer protects against stockouts.

SS = z·σL  |  ROP = R̄·L + SS

Parameters

Avg Daily Demand (R̄) 50 units/day
Expected demand per day.
Demand Std Dev (σd) 10 units/day
Day-to-day variability in demand.
Lead Time (L, days) 7 days
Time between placing an order and receiving it.
Service Level (%) 95%
Probability of not stocking out during lead time. Drives the z-score.
Adjust service level to see the exponential cost of perfection.
